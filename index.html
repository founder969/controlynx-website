The problem

Monday morning.
Every week. The same chaos.

Every construction project in the world runs on the same broken workflow. And every planning engineer knows exactly what we mean.

01
Data collection takes all morning

Your planning engineer spends Monday morning on WhatsApp and phone calls — chasing site teams and subcontractors for progress data that should have arrived automatically.

"I'll submit the report by 3pm — just waiting on data from site."

02
Reports are submitted with missing data

The data collection is never fully complete by the time the report is due. So every report contains estimates, gaps, and figures that are already outdated the moment they are submitted.

"I'll update the numbers when I get confirmation from the subcontractor."

03
40% of the week is spent on formatting

Planning engineers are hired to analyse schedules, identify risks, and protect the project programme. Instead, they spend nearly half their week copy-pasting data into Word and Excel templates.

"I didn't get time to review the programme this week — the monthly report took three days."

Daily Progress Report (DPR)

Auto-generated from site team inputs. Complete, formatted, and client-ready every morning before your engineer finishes their coffee.
